Menu

Shio nigiri is a simple yet savory classic among the many varieties of onigiri, Japanese rice balls. It is made with nothing more than steamed rice, salt, and often a strip of seaweed for wrapping.
Ume nigiri is a delightful and refreshing variation of onigiri, Japanese rice balls, that showcases the harmonious balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. It features steamed rice encasing a filling of pickled umeboshi, plums known for their tangy and slightly salty taste.
Konbu nigiri is a variation of Japanese rice balls, or onigiri, that is enhanced with the savory, umami flavor of kombu, a type of edible seaweed. The kombu is typically simmered in dashi, a Japanese soup stock, to extract its rich flavors and soften the seaweed.
Karage nigiri is a unique and flavorful variation of onigiri, Japanese rice balls, that features a crispy coating of karaage, deep-fried chicken pieces.
Tuna mayo nigiri is a delectable and popular variation of onigiri, Japanese rice balls, that features a creamy and savory filling of canned tuna mixed with mayonnaise and soy sauce.
Mentaiko nigiri is a bold and savory variation of onigiri, Japanese rice balls, that features a spicy and umami-packed filling of marinated pollock roe, or mentaiko.
